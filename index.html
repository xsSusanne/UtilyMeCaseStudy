The challenge

You suspect the bill is wrong. You don’t have a week to find out.

Verifying a German utility bill today means a Mieterbund consultation, a lawyer, or hours with a spreadsheet. For most tenants, none of those happen.

“I always feel like something is off, but I have neither the time nor the legal knowledge to actually check.”

The non-negotiable

UtilyMe is a tool, not a lawyer.

Every verdict carries a BetrKV disclaimer reviewed by external counsel. The product helps a tenant decide whether to investigate further — it does not replace Mieterbund. This is embedded in the Definition of Done, the Go/No-Go gates, and every line of public copy.

What I learned

Trust is built by what you choose not to do.

The hardest decisions were what to leave out. A dispute-letter generator, a recurring reminder, a premium tier — each tested well, each was deferred. Trust at MVP comes from doing one thing well.

Splitting the LLM and the deterministic math was the single most important architectural call. The LLM extracts; the math audits. A defensible verdict is the only verdict worth shipping.

User research is the spine, not the discovery activity. Every prioritisation conversation came back to what Anna, Markus and Priya said.

“You suspected. Now you know.”
